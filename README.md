# Stock Market Performance Analysis 2013–2018

## Overview
I built this project to stand out from other candidates applying for finance 
and analyst roles. Rather than just listing Excel and SQL on my CV, I wanted 
to actually show what I can do with them. I pulled 5 years of stock data 
across 10 companies, cleaned it, analysed it and built a dashboard that 
tells a clear story about market performance.

## What I Did
I took daily stock price data for 10 companies across Technology and Energy 
sectors, cleaned and structured it in Excel, wrote SQL queries to dig into 
the numbers and built an interactive Tableau dashboard to present the findings.

It covers the full process — messy raw data all the way through to something 
you can actually look at and understand quickly.

## Tools
- **Excel** — cleaned the raw data, calculated monthly returns, cumulative 
  returns and used conditional formatting to make performance patterns visible
- **SQL** — wrote queries to rank companies, count negative months, compare 
  sectors and join tables together
- **Tableau** — built a four chart interactive dashboard, published publicly

## Companies
**Technology:** Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Google, Facebook  
**Energy:** ExxonMobil, Chevron, NextEra Energy, ConocoPhillips, Halliburton

## What I Found
- Technology averaged 2.85% monthly return against Energy at 0.44% — 
  not even close over five years
- Nvidia was the standout performer across the whole dataset, highest 
  average return and biggest single month gain
- Energy stocks had noticeably more negative months — the volatility 
  was clear when you look at the heatmap
- Facebook had the highest profit margin at 78%, which surprised me 
  given how much money Meta spends
